Description:-

Cheese balls are a versatile, globally enjoyed treat that generally falls into two categories: a crispy, deep-fried appetizer with a gooey, melted-cheese center, or a light and airy extruded corn puff snack.

Ingredients:-

  • Main Cheese: Shredded Mozzarella or Cheddar cheese
  • Binding Agents: Boiled mashed potatoes or Corn flour
  • Coating Layer: Beaten eggs (or cornstarch slurry) and Breadcrumbs
  • Aromatics: Finely chopped garlic, green chilies, and coriander leaves
  • Spices: Black pepper powder, chili flakes, and mixed Italian herbs
  • Frying Medium: Cooking oil (for deep-frying)

Steps:-

  1. Combine: In a large mixing bowl, add 1 cup of grated mozzarella, ½ cup of grated cheddar, and 2 medium boiled, mashed potatoes (or 2 tablespoons of cornstarch for binding).
  2. Season: Add 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, ½ teaspoon of chili flakes, ½ teaspoon of mixed herbs, and a pinch of salt and black pepper.
  3. Mix: Knead everything together gently until it forms a smooth, non-sticky dough
  4. Divide: Grease your palms with a little oil.
  5. Roll: Take small portions of the dough and roll them into smooth, bite-sized balls (about the size of a lemon). Ensure there are no cracks on the surface, or the cheese will leak out while frying.
  6. Prepare Slurry: In a small bowl, whisk 2 tablespoons of cornstarch and 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour (maida) with ¼ cup of water to make a smooth, thin slurry.Prepare Breadcrumbs: Place 1 cup of breadcrumbs on a flat plate.Double Coat: Dip a cheese ball into the flour slurry, let the excess drip off, and then roll it generously in the breadcrumbs. For an extra-crispy shell that never bursts, dip it back into the slurry and roll it in the breadcrumbs a second time.
  7. Freeze: Place the coated cheese balls on a plate and freeze them for 20 to 30 minutes.Why? This firms up the cheese so it doesn't melt too quickly and explode in the hot oil.
  8. Heat Oil: Heat cooking oil in a deep pan on medium-high heat.Test: Drop a few breadcrumbs into the oil; if they sizzle and rise immediately, the oil is ready.
  9. Fry: Gently lower 4-5 cheese balls at a time into the hot oil. Fry for 2 to 3 minutes, turning gently, until they are uniformly golden brown and crisp.Drain: Remove them using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve piping hot with tomato ketchup or cheesy dip!
