Pasta

Home

Description:-

Pasta is a traditional Italian staple food made from an unleavened dough of wheat flour, water, or eggs. It is formed into sheets or various shapes and then cooked by boiling or baking.

Ingredients:-

  • Semolina Flour: 2 cups (preferably Durum wheat or "Tipo 00" flour)
  • Eggs: 3 large whole eggs (plus 1-2 extra yolks for richness)

Steps:-

  1. Mound: Pour 2 cups of flour (Durum semolina or "Tipo 00") onto a clean, large wooden board or countertop.Make a Hole: Use your fingers or a fork to clear a wide well in the center of the flour mound. It should look like a small volcano. Make sure the walls are thick enough to hold the liquids.
  2. Fill: Crack 3 large eggs into the center of the well. Add 1 teaspoon of olive oil and a small pinch of salt.Whisk: Use a fork to gently beat the eggs, oil, and salt together until they are completely blended. Be careful not to break the flour walls yet.
  3. Incorporate: Gradually use the fork to pull small amounts of flour from the inner walls of the well into the egg mixture.Combine: Keep whisking in a circular motion, bringing in more flour until a thick paste forms in the center.Form a Ball: Once the mixture becomes too thick for the fork, use your hands or a bench scraper to fold the remaining flour into the center. Bring everything together until it forms a shaggy, rough ball of dough. discard any stubborn, dry flour clumps left on the board.
  4. Stretch: Push the dough ball away from you using the heel of your hand.Fold: Fold the top edge back over, rotate the dough 90 degrees, and push away again.Repeat: Continue kneading vigorously for 8 to 10 minutes. The dough will initially feel stiff, but it will gradually transform into a smooth, elastic, and slightly shiny ball. If you press it with a finger, it should slowly bounce back.
  5. Wrap: Wrap the smooth dough ball tightly in plastic wrap (cling film) to prevent it from drying out.Rest: Leave it on the counter at room temperature for 30 minutes.Why? This relaxes the gluten in the flour, making the dough pliable and easy to roll out without it springing back.
